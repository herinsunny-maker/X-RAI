Understanding Your Diagnosis: The KL Grading System

Physicians use the Kellgren-Lawrence (KL) classification to measure the severity of Knee Osteoarthritis (OA). OA is a degenerative joint disease where the cartilage that cushions your joints wears down over time.

Grade 0: Normal No radiographic features of OA are present. Healthy joint space.
Grade 1: Doubtful Doubtful joint space narrowing and possible osteophysic (bone spur) lipping.
Grade 2: Mild Definite osteophytes and possible joint space narrowing.
Grade 3: Moderate Multiple moderate osteophytes, definite joint space narrowing, and some sclerosis (bone hardening).
Grade 4: Severe Large osteophytes, marked joint space narrowing, severe sclerosis, and definite deformity of bone ends.
